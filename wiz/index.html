    A System of Accountability and Transparency


    Establish a baseline for your community by completing a series of questions, based on themes in sustainability. The Blue Community Assessment app is a paperless process inside an easy to edit cloud-based virtual sustainability assessment ecosystem for destination communities. It allows the work of data collection, entry and update to be distributed across a team. All destinations are assigned a team color. When assessors log in, the system already knows that team they belong to and which criteria they are permitted to edit/update/access. Once all 133 questions have been given an initial answer, 100% of baseline will be complete. From that moment forward, everything is tracked based on updates provided to the assessment over time, activating Historical Data modules and such.  Add a Destination Community to the system here as a chatbot..

    No more greenwashing. All edits made to the destination data are time stamped and the change becomes attributed to the person making the change. This data is only viewable by the members belonging to the same team. This change becomes archived to our database and recorded as a new change within the blockchain. The blue community blockchain keeps a record of every change which happens to the data over time, beginning from the date baseline was established. We aim to challenge the negative impacts of tourism by providing a place where destination communities can collaboratively measure, report, track and analyze local sustainability efforts, as a team. Each team is assigned a color which represents the destination members of that team have permissions to edit, update or contribute to.

  • Give the Stakeholders a Reason to Trust You

    Help the community trust you by providing the transparency stakeholders seek. Along with providing reference links to claims made inside the assessment, you can upload photos, videos, documents and evidence. When your destination provides demonstrable proof of improvement and implementations, the public will have a way to see you accomplish your established goals in real time. Blue Community will help your team with your plan and everything you need to succeed with your team efforts. Each section has a place for recording "Notes to Stakeholders" This is where goals can be announced and visible to stakeholders of your community. It is very simple to record a number and providing proof isn't difficult either. It is the act of setting a goal to improve which moves the needle. It is knowing the answer to a question so well that you cannot wait for the new data to become available.

    Unlike many sustainability related assessments which require someone on a team to do most of the work, the blue community assessment app is meant to be edited as a team. Every team inside the blue community assessment is notated by a color that color team associate you with all of the data for your destinations. A team manager can easily assign different sections of the assessment to different people in the team depending on their expertise. This way team members will only have between five and 10 questions to keep track of overtime. Having the most current data is paramount to setting goals for your Blue Community. As the data becomes converted into graphs inside the Destination Dashboard, all representation of change over time will become exciting for your team, especially when the data proves the achievement of a goal.

  • Users are Assessors of Their Local Community

    We take the ability to edit data for a destination quite seriously. For this reason users of the assessment app will login with an email address that is associated with their team. By logging in as a team member associated with the team, you are able to easily edit sustainability data across all of the metrics without distractions. All data that is edited and submitted through the assessment app is reflected in real time to the Bluecommunities dashboard for stakeholders. One of the features that makes the blue community assessment much different than anything else like it is the focus on accountability and transparency. For this reason every edit made overtime to data related to a destination is archived and blocked chain for review at a later date if needed. By providing data in this transparent manner you will gain the trust of the stakeholders through the process of setting goals proclaiming actions and meeting those goals over time.

  • Blue Community Metrics

    Our metric groups are based around the most important sustainability related issues found in your destinations community, particularly those which have an impact on tourism. There are 20 metric groups each representing a theme of sustainability, such as water, food, energy, waste, etc. Each question inside each metric group is directly aligned with sustainable tourism criteria provided by the GSTC the global sustainable tourism Council, the United Nations sustainability development goals, and the 12 blue community strategies developed with the Walt Disney environmental and wildlife division.

    The assessment includes all of the questions that are that are part of the Global Sustainable Tourism Council (GSTC) destination criteria. With those particularly questions there is a link to the specific GSTC criteria and examples of the kind of evidence you might provide in your descriptions, attachments, or website url links.

A source may be a website url link or the name title and contact info for the source of the data. All questions in the assessment need to be answered before proceeding to the next step of completing the online sustainability management plan.
